Measures of central tendency

Measures of central tendency are statistics that describe the “center” or typical value in a dataset. The most common are the mean (average), median (middle value), and mode (most frequent value). These measures help us summarize a set of numbers with a single value that best represents the entire dataset. Additionally, quartiles divide the data into four parts, helping us understand its spread and identify outliers. By calculating these values, we gain insight into where most data points are concentrated and how the data is distributed around the center.
